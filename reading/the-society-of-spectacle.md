# The Society of the Spectacle
Guy Debord · 1967

> everything that was directly lived has moved away into a representation.

A dense, fragmentary book about how life under capitalism has become a performance. Debord argues that we no longer experience reality directly. We experience representations of reality  ;  images, advertisements, news, social media  ;  until the representation becomes more real than the thing itself.

The spectacle is not just media. It is a social relationship mediated by images. You do not go to the mountains anymore; you watch a video of the mountains. You do not have a conversation; you post a comment. Your life becomes something you curate for an audience, and the audience becomes the authority on what your life means.

Debord wrote this in 1967. He had never seen the internet. He had never seen an iPhone. And yet he described Instagram, TikTok, influencer culture, and the entire attention economy before any of it existed. That is either brilliant or terrifying. Probably both.

I think the best part of the book is how it reframes the problem. We think the issue with social media is that we spend too much time on it. Debord would say the issue is that we have accepted a life of passive spectatorship. The opposite of the spectacle is not better media. It is direct experience.
