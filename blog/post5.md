# Mindset shift, I think?
Blog · July 2026
topics: devlog, culture

I am not that interesting, and so is other people. Maybe I am, but I guess its good to not take yourself too seriously and other people too seriously too.

In social settings, yes. Fun conversations are meant to be light and non-serious in nature. In business & trade, being interesting is not the goal. Trust, labour and money, in that order is more important. If you have the trust of the people doing business with you, you'll be in a good position. You do your work, you have a trade. When you have your trade, then you will earn money.

It's a given that even light and fun conversations can give you insights, learn behaviours but never skills. You can tell what kind of person you're dealing with, listening is a very very useful skill to have. Funny reading the room usually comes down to change of tone more than looking at body language.

I've come to realise labelling people according to their positions, their divisions, their responsibilities, are just how business works. It's for you to profile how to handle them, not to attribute the person with the position itself (as in character). Although there are tendencies, but relying on those will be unsavory as you scale further. That's what I learn.

It's almost always better to learn yourself and how you could handle most situations, people, everything in between. That's the beauty of it isn't it?
