# Letters to a Young Poet
Rainer Maria Rilke · 1929

> live your way into the answer.

A collection of letters Rilke wrote to a young officer who sent him poetry asking for criticism. Instead of giving craft advice, Rilke tells him something more important: do not write because you want to be a poet. Write because you have to. If you can live without writing, then do something else.

The book is about more than poetry. It is about how to live as a sensitive person in a world that does not reward sensitivity. Rilke talks about solitude not as loneliness but as a discipline. He tells the young poet to be patient with everything unresolved in his heart and to love the questions themselves.

There is a tenderness in these letters. Rilke takes the young man seriously. He does not patronize him. He speaks to him like an equal who simply has more years. That alone is a lesson.

I think the best part of the book is the idea that you must live your way into the answers. Nobody can give you the answers. You have to grow into them. That applies to poetry, to work, to love, to everything.
