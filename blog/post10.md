# To Question is To Human
Blog · July 2026
topics: technology, philosophy

Make no mistake, questions are essential to everything understanding, and understanding everything (in the scope of what it is to be understood). Therefore, question everyday, not everything, but things not yet understood. I have been in and out with AI, with the downside seems to outweigh the good, or maybe rather, the goods are yet unexplored?

It is easy to prompt an app into existence, if the app already exist. Easy to think that we created something, cause our ego tell us so. "I prompted this with my own hands, therefore it is my hardwork!!". Only an egoistical person would see such a minute labor and call it hardwork. Step back and ask, what the values of the product you prompt into existence, what are the problems that you are solving and most importantly, does the solution work? Oh, the saddest thing about AI is probably the layoffs, dumb CEO's are basing their decisions on chatbots. Give me a break. Real people with real livelihood are at stake.

Please remember, no matter how capable an AI model is, they are just advance auto-complete technology.

People seem to forget, that despite everything being flavoured with 'legal', 'finance', 'entertainment', in the end its all a transfer of information from point A to point B, through medium x and content y. That runs the world. Everything is just advance pen and paper, while pen and paper is advance clay tablets and stylus. They did spreadsheet back then, and we still do it now. All in the name of trade. Making sure trade goes smoothly, all these information technology exist.

Cars and roads, bullets and guns, wind and blowpipes, cement and houses. The system are our own creation, and these tools are the extension of ourselves. Everything is just, information. Visual, audio, physical, smell, taste.

I hope that make sense. My understanding of the modern world comes from a book by Marshall McLuhan, Understanding Media. As you are reading this with your eyes, remember, I was typing this with my fingers, some amount of time, before.
