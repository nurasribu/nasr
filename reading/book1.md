# Understanding Media
Marshall McLuhan · 1964

> The medium is the message.

Understanding Media : The Extension of Man by Marshall McLuhan is a media theory book explaining and demistifying the world of information mediums, from ancient tablets to the movie screen. It converts the physical capacity of these mediums into abstract explanations to describe what exactly the function and effects of these industry.

When someone mentions 'media' we would associate the word with the news, journalist, such and such. The technology itself varies and the effect of those variety are explained in such a way that readers could grasp the significance of media in society, governance and personal experience.

Shared reality through information in the form of video(visual), radio(sound), books (audio in visual form), the relationship between legislation and the printed form, human coordination and governance. Optics and politics, advertisement and marketing. 

The masters (owner of media company) and how they manage human perception through the platforms that they have, censorship and human reactions. All explained in a very minute abstract way.

For example, the concept of hot and cold media, hot being something explosive and cold being something that pacify the people. The business of subtle 'make know' and not 'make aware'; to control and how to handle a reactive population.

This is a good read if you want to understand the significance of information sharing, the technology and the money surrounding it, the perception of time and reality because of media, the American imperialism and Hollywood psyops.

I think, the best part of the book is its released in 1964, but it somehow predicted a society with access to unlimited information (the internet and social media) very well. It does not mention explicitly, but somehow the explanation on the effects of colored TV and movies, the radio, and how art reacted to technology is somewhat like a foresight.
