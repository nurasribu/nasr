# Early Reflection - On AI
Blog · July 2026
topics: technology, philosophy

Let's step back for a second. Technology had enabled industries and people. Anything goes. Especially when we're living in the midst of the world of AI, anything can pretty much scale in a meaningful way. Scale is no longer a problem. Although it gets, uncomfortable sometimes. Therefore, it is important to understand the very basis of why, what, how things are the way things are.

Scale is no longer scarce. Be greatful of that. For me, I would focus on expanding my depth instead.

AI enabled a the highest level of abstraction to be filled with a placeholder. Have you ever generated an AI template with the plan originally to replace parts of it with your own words, but as you're reading the AI output, you're just stuck and out of words?

Whatever happened there, it seems like somehow, AI is good at doing things at a higher 'base' level than humans. That is a good thing. What AI lacks and what AI moghuls won't tell you is the disingenuity of the tool, while being fully capable to do stuffs, surface level stuff. What AI lacks, we as humans do best.

I think there's a cap where AI stops being any significantly better, as for now, use what we have. Lookout on the Chinese AI companies, they understand consumers better. While the Americans are pushing the boundaries of cloud AI services and Data Centres, the Chinese are installing AI labs inside consumers home.

I just hope RAM prices to fall, and water shouldn't be scarced.
