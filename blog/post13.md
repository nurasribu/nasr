# If The Audience Is Watching
Blog · July 2026
topics: philosophy, mentalmodes

If you're a character starring in a movie, what would the audience be screaming at you right now? That's exactly what you should do right now. I am guilty of doomscrolling, having found my edge (my company's advantage), proven the technology via early prototyping, and developing a concept of the tech's development, now comes to a stage where I have to be more elaborate in my approach, which is the capital stage.

Okay lets decompress, there's 3 things for a startup to happen, the strategy, the capital, the execution. 

I am at the stage where I have to demistify capital very fast before competitors find out the edge and implement it themselves. The world is moving faster than it ever was before. If you're slacking of someone out there is already figuring out steps to your edge.

In this current moment, I feel slightly uncomfortable with the idea that my idea is a viable idea, and a viable strategy exist and I know that strategy and I can actually test it. That's outside of my comfort zone. The audience must be exhilerating right now, seeing me wasting away my potential again. I am lucky that the context of my business can work theoretically and there's a path for me that opens. 

While I am writing down the business strategy, timeline, capital needed, I can't help but feel nervous and think of the scenario that I should not focus on yet. Those are tomorrow's problem. What am I worried about? Today's problem is today's problem, and those are the things I should immediately settle before I think of other problems that might and might not happen in the future.
