# The Lean Startup
Eric Ries · 2011

> the only way to win is to learn faster than anyone else.

This book changed how startups are built. Before it, the standard approach was to write a business plan, raise money, build a product, and hope people wanted it. Ries argues that this is backwards. You should build a minimal product, test it with real customers, learn what works, and iterate.

The core concept is the Build-Measure-Learn loop. Build something small. Measure how people use it. Learn what to do next. Repeat. The goal is not to execute a plan perfectly. The goal is to find out what the plan should be.

Ries draws on lean manufacturing principles from Toyota. Just as Toyota reduced waste on the factory floor, startups should reduce waste in product development. The biggest waste is building something nobody wants.

I think the best part of the book is the validation of small experiments. You do not need to bet everything on one big launch. You can test a hypothesis with a landing page and some ads. That is freeing. It takes the pressure off. You are not trying to be right. You are trying to learn.
