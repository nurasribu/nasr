# 1984
George Orwell · 1949

> Big Brother is watching you.

Everyone knows the terms: Big Brother, doublethink, thought police, newspeak. But the book is not just a political warning. It is a study of what happens when reality becomes a thing that can be manufactured.

Orwell builds a world where the Party controls not just what people do, but what they remember. If the past is erased, there is no standard to measure the present against. You cannot rebel against a system you cannot prove is lying. That is the real horror of it. Not the torture. The erasure.

The book follows Winston Smith, a low-ranking party member who begins to question. His rebellion is small  ;  he buys a diary, he writes down his thoughts  ;  but that is enough to destroy him. The ending is not heroic. That is what makes it real.

I think the best part of the book is how it predicted the way information would be weaponized. Not the surveillance state exactly, but the manipulation of language. Newspeak was supposed to narrow the range of thought by shrinking the vocabulary. We do the same thing now with slogans, with algorithms, with the way we talk past each other.
